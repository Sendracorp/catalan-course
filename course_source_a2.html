How to use this course (A2)

This is the A2 (Bàsic) course — it continues straight on from A1. We assume you can already read Catalan aloud from the IPA, use the present tense, and handle greetings, numbers and basic descriptions. A2 adds the past tenses, the future and conditional, weak and combined pronouns, and the vocabulary and functions you need to narrate, describe and give opinions — and to pass the official Certificat de nivell bàsic (A2).

Quick IPA refresher: A2 assumes the A1 sound system. The essentials below are all you need on this page; the full guide lives in the A1 course.

Vowels

Central Catalan has eight vowels. The one that catches everyone: unstressed a and e both become the schwa /ə/, and unstressed o becomes /u/. Stressed e/o are open /ɛ ɔ/ or closed /e o/.

Consonants & habits — the golden rule

Written v = /b/; b, d, g soften between vowels /β ð ɣ/; the final -r of infinitives is silent (parlar /pəɾˈla/); x = /ʃ/, ll = /ʎ/, ny = /ɲ/.

ser vs estar

Use ser for identity, origin, profession and permanent traits (Soc de Lleida I'm from Lleida); use estar for location, temporary states and how you feel (Estic cansat I'm tired). The auxiliary haver builds the perfet you'll learn in Unit 3.

Es retroben — Catching up

— Hola, Marc! Quant de temps! Com estàs? Hi, Marc! Long time! How are you?

— Molt bé, gràcies. Ara treballo en una llibreria i també estudio català. Very well, thanks. Now I work in a bookshop and I'm also studying Catalan.

— Ah, doncs parles molt bé! I on vius ara? Ah, so you speak really well! And where do you live now?

— Visc a Girona, però soc de Reus. I tu, què fas? I live in Girona, but I'm from Reus. And you, what do you do?

Unit 2

People & Descriptions

By the end you can: describe what people look like and what they're like, and compare them — a staple of the A2 speaking and writing tasks.

Adjectives: agreement & position

Adjectives agree in gender and number and usually come after the noun: un noi alt a tall boy, una noia alta, uns nois alts, unes noies altes. Most add -a for the feminine; many ending in -e or a consonant like -ent don't change (alegre, intel·ligent).

Comparing

més … que more … than · menys … que less … than · tan … com as … as. Superlative: el/la més …. Irregular: bo → millor good → better, dolent → pitjor bad → worse.

Qui és aquell? — Who's that?

— Qui és aquell noi alt dels cabells rossos? Who's that tall boy with blond hair?

— És en Pau. És molt simpàtic, però una mica tímid. That's Pau. He's very nice, but a bit shy.

— I la noia? És més alta que ell. And the girl? She's taller than him.

— És la seva germana. És tan simpàtica com ell. She's his sister. She's as nice as he is.

Unit 3

The Recent Past — el perfet

By the end you can: say what you have done today and recently — the first past tense and a frequent A2 listening/speaking target.

How to form the perfet

The perfet = present of haver + the past participle. Use it for the recent past, especially within today or "so far".

haver+ participleEnglish
heparlatI have spoken
hasmenjatyou have eaten
habeguthe/she has drunk
hemdormitwe have slept
heuvistyou (pl) have seen
hanfetthey have done

Participles

Regular: -ar → -at (parlat), -re/-er → -ut (perdut, begut), -ir → -it (dormit). Key irregulars: fer → fet, dir → dit, veure → vist, escriure → escrit, obrir → obert, prendre → pres, venir → vingut.

Time markers that love the perfet: avui today, aquest matí this morning, ja already, encara no not yet, mai (n)ever.

Què has fet avui? — What have you done today?

— Què has fet avui? What have you done today?

— He treballat al matí i he dinat amb la Júlia. I tu? I worked in the morning and had lunch with Júlia. And you?

— Encara no he fet res! Però ja he comprat el regal. I haven't done anything yet! But I've already bought the present.

Unit 4

Narrating the Past — el passat perifràstic

By the end you can: narrate finished past events — what you did yesterday, last week, on your trip. This is the everyday "past simple" of Catalan.

How to form it

Catalan's most common past for finished events is the passat perifràstic: a special form of anar + the infinitive. (It looks like "to go", but it means "did".)

Auxiliary+ infinitiveEnglish
vaigparlarI spoke
vasmenjaryou ate
vabeurehe/she drank
vamdormirwe slept
vauveureyou (pl) saw
vanferthey did

perfet vs perifràstic: use the perfet for today/recent (avui he menjat); use the perifràstic for finished, dated past (ahir vaig menjar). Markers: ahir yesterday, la setmana passada last week, l'any passat last year, fa dos dies two days ago.

El cap de setmana — The weekend

— Què vas fer el cap de setmana? What did you do at the weekend?

— Vaig anar a Girona i vaig visitar la catedral. I vosaltres? I went to Girona and visited the cathedral. And you?

— Vam quedar a casa i vam veure una pel·lícula. We stayed home and watched a film.

Unit 5

The Way Things Were — l'imperfet

By the end you can: describe past habits, settings and how things used to be — "when I was little", "we always used to…".

How to form the imperfet

Regular endings: -ar → -ava (parlava), and -re/-er/-ir → -ia (perdia, dormia). Use it for habits, repeated actions and description in the past (age, weather, feelings, scenes).

Pronounparlardormir
joparlavadormia
tuparlavesdormies
ell/ellaparlavadormia
nosaltresparlàvemdormíem
vosaltresparlàveudormíeu
ells/ellesparlavendormien

Key irregulars: ser → era, fer → feia, veure → veia, dir → deia. Markers: abans before/used to, sempre always, quan era petit/a when I was little, cada dia every day.

Quan era petita — When I was little

— Quan eres petita, on vivies? When you were little, where did you live?

— Vivia a un poble petit. Cada estiu anàvem a la platja. I lived in a small village. Every summer we went to the beach.

— Que bé! Jo sempre jugava al carrer amb els amics. How nice! I always used to play in the street with friends.
